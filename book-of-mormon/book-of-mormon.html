Evidences of the Book of Mormon

Another Testament of Jesus Christ — Converging Evidence
Multidisciplinary Support

Linguistic Evidence

  • Chiasmus (Alma 36)

    Complex ancient Hebrew poetry form (A-B-C-C-B-A). The entire chapter of Alma 36 reverses itself, centering on Jesus Christ.1

  • Hebraisms

    Cognate Accusative ("Dreamed a dream") and "If/And" conditionals common in Hebrew but awkward in English, found in the original manuscript.

  • Ancient Names

    "Alma" (confirmed as a male Hebrew name in Bar Kokhba letters, 130 AD) and "Jershon" (Root YRSH "to inherit").2

  • Uto-Aztecan & Semitic Parallels

    Linguist Brian Stubbs documents over 1,500 consistent sound shifts between Uto-Aztecan languages and Semitic/Egyptian.3

Archaeological Evidence

  • Nahom (Old World)

    Altars found in Yemen inscribed with the tribal name "NHM," dating to Lehi's time (600-700 BC), in the precise location described.4

  • Bountiful (Wadi Sayq)

    Khor Kharfot in Oman matches all 12 criteria (fresh water, timber, ore, harbor) in a region thought to be barren by 19th-century critics.

  • Hopewell Fortifications

    Alma 50 describes earth banks + ditches + timber pickets. This matches the exact construction of Hopewell defensive works in the US Midwest.

  • Bat Creek Stone

    Smithsonian find (1889) in Tennessee. Identified by Dr. Cyrus Gordon as Paleo-Hebrew ("For Judea"), dating to the Nephite period.5

Genetic Evidence

  • West Eurasian Ancestry

    2013 Nature study (Mal'ta Boy) confirms 14-38% of Native American ancestry derives from ancient Western Eurasian populations.6

  • Haplogroup X2a

    A mitochondrial DNA lineage found in the Near East (Druze) and Native Americans (Great Lakes/Hopewell region), distinct from primary Asian migrations.

  • Population Drift

    Genetic bottlenecking and drift would naturally dilute markers of a small founder group (Lehi's family) within a larger existing population.

Cultural & Historical

  • Oral Histories

    Widespread Native American traditions of a "Great White God" or "Pale Prophet" who healed the sick and taught peace (parallels 3 Nephi).

  • Destruction Legends

    Tribal accounts of cataclysmic thunder and days of darkness, aligning with the destruction described at Christ's death.

  • Transoceanic Migration

    Legends (e.g., Hopi) of ancestors crossing a "great water" or ocean to arrive, contradicting the exclusive land-bridge theory.

  • Translation Timeline

    Text dictated in approx. 65-75 working days by an uneducated farmer with no notes. Distinct stylometry for different authors.7
