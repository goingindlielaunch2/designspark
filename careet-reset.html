2026 Career Reset Strategy

Positioning for Product Output, AI Orchestration, and Design-Engineering in the January 2026 market.

1. Market Context (January 2026)

The Northern Utah tech corridor (“Silicon Slopes”) has matured. While enterprise anchors like Adobe and Ancestry remain, growth is concentrated in mid-market SaaS and HealthTech across Lehi, Provo, and Salt Lake City.

  • The AI Filter: Junior-level production work is largely automated. Companies now hire for Design-Engineers and AI-Augmented Designers. Practical experience using Gemini, Claude, and AI-native IDEs is a strong senior-signal.
  • The Utah Factor: Hybrid work is favored over full-remote. Physical proximity to Ogden–Provo is a meaningful advantage.
  • Salary Reality: $80k is the realistic mid-level target. $50k undervalues 10+ years of cumulative experience, even with a gap.

2. Honest Assessment

Strengths

  • Swiss Army Knife Profile: Design → Code fluency (Figma → React/Hydrogen).
  • AI-Native Workflow: Demonstrated 3× output using AI tooling.
  • Industry Breadth: Healthcare, e-commerce, and B2B SaaS experience.

Friction Points

  • 16-Month Gap: Must be framed as a Product Lab, not unemployment.
  • Fragmented Narrative: Requires role-specific resumes.
  • Support Identity Drift: Must re-anchor as Lead Product Builder.

3. 30-Day Execution Plan

Week 1 – Foundation & AI-First Brand

  • Create two resumes: UX/Product and Front-End Engineer.
  • Reframe gap as “Founder & Lead Designer – Product Lab.”
  • Portfolio: 3 projects only (AI app, Shopify/React build, Design System).

Week 2 – Hidden Utah Market

  • Focus on HealthTech and E-commerce SaaS.
  • Warm outreach to Lead Designers and Eng Managers.
  • Identify 10 Tier-1 local companies.

Week 3 – Proof of Work

  • Apply to only 10 roles using audits or value videos.
  • Run AI UX audits on target company sites.
  • Daily TypeScript reinforcement.

Week 4 – Interview & Conversion

  • Rehearse Product Lab narrative.
  • Pivot to contract-to-hire if needed ($60–70/hr).
